No one will ever know the violence it took to your memories having expiration date

No one will ever know the violence it took to your memories having expiration date. Such a sentence hit him when least needed, a falling inevitable rain of cold stinging water; true, nonetheless. Honestly, this sentence walked past most of his life unnoticed. Fortunately, life keeps track of your to do-list and checks boxes for you. So, thank you. If I could leave you with anything I would leave you with this devil’s phrase “timing is all that’s needed, yet timing is bullshit in the wind”: you smelt it far away but you never expect where/when the uppercut is coming from.

Despite the devil surely could be behind timing, my dear is not a religious believer. “I once was”, he seems to think, an intrusive though like those he could barely control. “I wish I continued to be”- he murmured while lowering his eyes towards the solid pavement. Certainly, it would make easier the lonely walk he is immersed into, much calmer the thoughts he fed, starving dark hounds waiting, always waiting; stalking from the reptilian brain, unbothered, out-of-reach from the conscious himself. He once was a believer of an overall powerful smith that tailors every soul, a drawer that defines the destiny, a knitter that connects, hence a god. I won’t mention the god’s name for copyright purposes. You know it, it’s enough.

He once was a believer. Not anymore.
